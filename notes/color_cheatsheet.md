# UI/UX Color Palette Cheat Sheet (2025)

This cheat sheet summarizes the core principles of accessibility, psychology, and strategic implementation for digital interfaces.

---

### 1. Composition: The 60-30-10 Rule

Balance your interface to reduce cognitive load and create visual hierarchy.

* **60% Neutral (Dominant):** Backgrounds and large surfaces. Use "off-whites" or "soft greys" to prevent eye strain.
* **30% Brand (Secondary):** Headers, sidebars, and supporting brand elements.
* **10% Accent (Action):** Reserved strictly for CTAs, progress bars, and critical interactions.

### 2. Accessibility & Inclusivity

Design for all users by following **WCAG 2.1** standards.

* **Contrast Ratios:** * **4.5:1** for normal text.
* **3:1** for large text and UI components.


* **Beyond Color:** Never use color as the *only* indicator of information (e.g., use an icon + red text for errors).
* **Grayscale Test:** If your design doesn't work in black and white, your contrast hierarchy is failing.

### 3. Accent Strategy (The Isolation Effect)

Based on the **Von Restorff Effect**, which states that the "different" item is the one users remember and act upon.

* **The "Squint Test":** Squint at your screen; the accent color should be the only thing that stands out.
* **Functional vs. Decorative:** Use your accent color to "teach" the user what is interactive.
* **Consistency:** Use the same accent color for the same function across the entire journey.

### 4. Color Psychology & Semantics

Colors carry subconscious meaning and functional status.

* **Blue:** Trust, Security (Fintech, Healthcare).
* **Red:** Urgency, Errors, Danger.
* **Green:** Success, Growth, Confirmation.
* **Yellow/Orange:** Warnings, Notifications, Energy.
* **Semantic Colors:** These must override brand colors for status updates (e.g., a "Success" message should usually be green even if your brand is purple).

### 5. 2025 Trends & Best Practices

* **Tinted Neutrals:** Use a 2% tint of your accent color in your greys to create "Color Harmony."
* **Calm Tech:** Shift toward desaturated, earthy tones and "Moonlit Greys" to combat digital fatigue.
* **Dark Mode Optimization:** Desaturate colors for dark backgrounds to prevent "visual vibration" and eye strain.
